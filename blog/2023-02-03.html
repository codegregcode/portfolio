03/02/23 - Bootcamp/blog beginnings

I started this blog to keep track of my progress whilst on the Command Shift fast track software development course. I initially tried to keep track on Twitter using #100daysofcode, but it wasn't working out as I wasn't into Twitter and found it hard to remember to add to it daily.

My name is Greg and I'm from Devon, but currently living in Leeds. After graduating from Leeds College of Music with a degree in music performance, I played in function bands at weddings and events. However, I preferred to play my own music, which didn't pay as much as playing in function bands. I then worked in a bank, but the inflexible hours made it hard for me to take time off for gigs. So, I quit and became a teaching assistant in a school. This gave me the perfect balance of time off at weekends and during the summer for gigs and festivals with my band, Mansion of Snakes!

After a few years, I wanted to make more money and obtained a TEFL qualification to teach English as a foreign language from home. I moved back to Leeds in August 2020, and when I did that, the TEFL wage went down and bookings disappeared, so I got a job as a playworker and a teaching assistant in a school.

Feeling stuck in a rut, and not playing any gigs, I decided to start coding during the summer of 2022. I began with myfreecodecamp, learning HTML and CSS and then JavaScript. A friend recommended that I join a bootcamp, so I chose Command Shift (then known as Manchester Codes) due to its part-time schedule, good reviews, and students moving into tech jobs quickly after graduating.

I joined the October 2022 cohort and was nervous at first, but quickly felt at ease. The course started with learning CLI, git, HTML and CSS, and having a tutor and other students around was helpful. The next module was JavaScript basics and things started to get a bit more challenging. But, I enjoyed the challenge, especially building virtual pets and cruise ships applications! Nevertheless, I'm eager to keep progressing through the course and excited to see where it takes me.
