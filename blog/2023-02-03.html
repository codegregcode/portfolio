HomeBlog Index 03/02/23 - Bootcamp/blog beginnings

I’ve started this blog, as a way to keep track on how I’m progressing through Command Shift fast track software dev course.

I initially started keeping track on twitter using the #100daysofcode, but I’ve never really been into twitter, and trying to remember to add to it everyday just wasn’t happening.

So, that’s why I’ve started this blog!

I’m going to set myself 1 hour a week to write up what I’ve been up to! But for this post I’m going to give a quick overview of who I am, what I’ve done and how I’ve found the course.

My name is Greg, and I’m originally from Devon, but currently living in Leeds. I moved up here to study music performance at Leeds College of Music. After graduating I spent some time playing in function bands at weddings and different events etc. However, I never really enjoyed playing the types of events where you have to play other people’s music, I’d much rather play my own. Unfortunately, for me, people will pay you much more money to play ‘Uptown Funk’ or ‘Mr Brightside’, than for playing own, so if I was to stop playing functions/weddings, then I’d need a job.

I had no idea what I wanted to do ‘job wise’, and spent a few months working for a bank. And although the pay was good, the hours were not flexible. I was having to take more and more time off so I could play gigs. Then, when I had a full summer schedule of festivals, and I couldn’t take any more time off, I quit. I saw a job advert for working in a school, and the more I thought about it the more I thought that a school job (as a teaching assistant) would work well around my schedule.

So, I went from working in a bank, to working in a school, and the first thing I noticed was the difference in pay! However, I could get over that, as the freedom of having weekends and evening consistently free to gig kept me happy enough.

I did this for a few years, and eventually the lack of financial reimbursement was getting to me. I did some research and found out that you could make a decent wage teaching English as a foreign language from home! I decided to concentrate on my studies back in Devon, and got my TEFL qualification and started teaching. The flexibility of teaching from anywhere with an internet connection was great, and I was getting good money for (what seemed to me) very easy work.

After a few months in Devon I wanted to move back to Leeds. So I moved back up August 2020, and to supplement my income I got a job as a playworker in a school. Shortly after moving back to Leeds the TEFL wage went down, then the bookings started disappearing. I found out, after looking online, that many of these companies over hired during the pandemic, and coupled with rules in China about teaching children outside of school hours, the work stopped. Luckily, as I was working in a school as playworker (for an after school club) I was quickly able to get a job as teaching assistant. The exact job I left two years prior, just in a different school.

The pay was never very good in a school, and that hadn’t changed. In fact, the wage hadn’t changed at all, and as it hadn’t risen with inflation, it’s actually been cut! Also, during covid my band went on an indefinite hiatus due to members having babies and moving countries.

I’m still currently working at the school, but without the gigs keeping me happy, I’ve felt stuck in a rut. At the beginning of the summer holiday in 2022, I decided that I was going to do something that I’d be recommend to do back when I moved to Devon, coding!

My coding journey began by signing up to myfreecodecamp, and going through the HTML and CSS course, which I really enjoyed! Then it was onto the JavaScript one and I enjoyed that too! A good friend of mine recommend that I join a bootcamp, as that was what they’d done and it had been life changing for them.

There are lots of lots of results when you search for ‘coding bootcamp uk’, and I spent a lot of time looking at different prospectuses before deciding on Command Shift (then known as Manchester Codes).

The things that drew me to Command Shift, were that it was part time, it had lots of good reviews and a lots of students moving into tech quickly after graduating!

So I joined the October 2022 cohort!

I was slightly nervous when it came to the first lecture, but that quickly disappeared a few minutes into the class. After a few ice breaking exercises we got into the course proper, which started with learning about CLI, git, HTML and CSS.

Although I had done the freecodecamp HTML and CSS course, having an actual tutor, and other students, around was really helpful, and I found the foundations module to be a good way to ease myself into the routines of night classes twice a week and studying every night.

The next module was JavaScript basics, and things started to get a little more difficult. I had written plenty of functions in JS before on freecodecamp and code wars, but I’d never written any tests, or built something using JS from scratch! However, being in the same boat as a lot of the other students, and having really supportive tutors, really helped! Before long I was making my own tamagotchi-style virtual pet, which was a lot of fun, and a good chance to put everything we had learnt about object oriented programming and test driven development to use! The next project was cruise ships which was similar to virtual pets in that it uses TDD and OOP, but this time there were different classes (objects) which had to interact with one another AND there was also the extra challenge of adding a graphical user interface as well!

Two weeks into the new year and the building blocks module was finished, and I had built a website, an application that works on CLI as well as another application that has a GUI! There was a weeks break, and in that time I decided to start another project called across the universe. The idea with this application is that the user creates a universe, which contains galaxies, solar systems, planets and life! It’s similar to the cruise ships application, as across the universe will be using TDD and OOP with multiple classes interacting with one another.

Which brings us to now, today 3rd Feb 2023. And we’re in the backend module. I’m learning lots about docker, containers, SQL, pgAdmin. I’ve just finished writing some SQL for some queries on a database, which if you asked me to do a week ago, I’d have had no idea what you were talking about or where to start (or begin)!
